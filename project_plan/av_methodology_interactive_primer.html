Evaluating Emerging AV Dynamics with SF-CHAMP

A comprehensive review of SFCTA's activity-based modeling and dynamic network simulation framework. This application deconstructs the shift from conventional GIS to programmatic spatial pipelines.

1. The Methodology Shift

Traditional transportation modeling relies on static trip-based analysis and manual GUI-driven GIS tasks. This section outlines the project's pivot toward automated, reproducibility-first pipelines using Python and activity-based tour modeling.

Conventional Methods

  • Spatial

    Manual GUI GIS (ArcGIS/Shapefiles) with non-topological joins and static buffering.

  • Statistical

    Aggregate trip-based models. Individual movements analyzed in isolation without chaining.

  • Economic

    "Rule of Half" for consumer surplus—struggles with non-linear multi-modal shifts.

Project Methodology

  • Spatial

    Programmatic pipelines (GeoPandas, OSMnx). Automated graph-based street network correction.

  • Statistical

    Tour-based SF-CHAMP. Activity chains reflecting true human behavior via Random Utility Maximization.

  • Economic

    Disaggregate Logsums. Captures societal benefits across heterogeneous demographics.

Rationale: This shift ensures algorithmic transparency and the ability to process high-velocity AV telemetry, though it introduces significant computational overhead compared to legacy methods.

The Logsum Model (Consumer Surplus)

The logsum calculates the expected maximum utility across the entire choice set. It acts as a monetized measure of societal benefit.

E(CSn) = (1/αn) * ln(Σ eVnj) + C
Broad Expectations in Results:
  • Adding AVs typically increases the Logsum initially by providing a new choice.
  • If AV cruising increases traffic, transit utility (Vj) drops, potentially lowering the net Logsum.

The GTFS "Jump" Filter

Automatically discards GPS anomalies where bus speeds exceed 65 mph or distance jumps >500m/sec, ensuring model inputs are physically realistic.

Spatial Projection QA

Rule 2 programmatically enforces NAD83 UTM Zone 10N, preventing "out-of-county" data drifts that often plague manual GIS merges.

Episode Harmonization

Collapses multi-entity reports of the same crash (Developer vs Operator reports) into a single analytical unit to prevent over-counting.
